About

I am an economist–statistician working at the intersection of health economics, biostatistics, and public policy. My research focuses on generating policy-relevant evidence that can withstand serious scrutiny, using trial-based and quasi-experimental methods, predictive modelling, and cost–benefit analysis. I have worked extensively with linked administrative and clinical datasets across health, labour, housing, and criminal justice, and I regularly translate complex empirical findings for policymakers, industry partners, the public, and the courts.

In my current role at UNSW’s Nura Gili Centre for Indigenous Programs, I work on Indigenous-led co-design, implementation, and evaluation of health and social programs, integrating rigorous quantitative methods with community governance and lived experience. I have led large cross-disciplinary projects, mentored research teams, served as an independent expert witness, and am committed to transparent methods, reproducible code, and empirical work that genuinely informs decisions rather than decorates them.
